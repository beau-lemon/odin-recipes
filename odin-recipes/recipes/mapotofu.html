Mapo Tofu

Mapo Tofu is a popular Chinese dish from Sichuan province. It consists of tofu set in a spicy sauce, typically a thin, oily, and bright red suspension, based on doubanjiang (fermented broad bean and chili paste) and douchi (fermented black beans), along with minced meat, usually pork or beef. The dish is often topped with chopped green onions and Sichuan peppercorns, which give it a unique numbing and spicy flavor. Mapo Tofu is known for its bold flavors and is a favorite among those who enjoy spicy food.

  1. 1 block of tofu, cut into cubes
  2. 200g ground pork or beef
  3. 2 tablespoons doubanjiang (fermented broad bean and chili paste)
  4. 1 tablespoon douchi (fermented black beans), chopped
  5. 2 cloves garlic, minced
  6. 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  7. 1 cup chicken broth
  8. 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  9. 1 teaspoon sugar
  10. 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  11. Chopped green onions and Sichuan peppercorns for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and ginger, and sauté until fragrant.
  2. Add the ground pork or beef to the pan and cook until browned.
  3. Stir in the doubanjiang and douchi, and cook for another minute to release their flavors.
  4. Pour in the chicken broth, soy sauce, and sugar. Bring the mixture to a simmer.
  5. Gently add the cubed tofu to the pan, being careful not to break it. Simmer for about 5-10 minutes, allowing the tofu to absorb the flavors of the sauce.
  6. Garnish with chopped green onions and Sichuan peppercorns before serving. Enjoy your Mapo Tofu!
