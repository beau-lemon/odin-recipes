Ramen

Ramen is a Japanese noodle soup dish that has become popular worldwide. It typically consists of Chinese-style wheat noodles served in a meat or fish-based broth, often flavored with soy sauce or miso, and uses toppings such as sliced pork (chashu), nori (dried seaweed), menma (bamboo shoots), and scallions. Ramen comes in various regional styles, each with its own unique broth and toppings. Some of the most popular types of ramen include Shoyu (soy sauce-based), Miso (fermented soybean paste-based), Shio (salt-based), and Tonkotsu (pork bone-based). Ramen is enjoyed for its rich flavors and comforting qualities, making it a beloved dish for many people around the world.

  1. 200g ramen noodles
  2. 4 cups chicken or pork broth
  3. 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  4. 1 tablespoon miso paste (optional)
  5. 1 clove garlic, minced
  6. 1 teaspoon ginger, minced
  7. 100g sliced pork (chashu) or chicken
  8. 1 boiled egg, halved
  9. 1 sheet nori (dried seaweed), cut into strips
  10. 1/4 cup menma (bamboo shoots)
  11. 2 scallions, chopped

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, heat the broth and add the soy sauce, miso paste (if using), minced garlic, and ginger. Bring to a simmer and let it cook for about 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  2. While the broth is simmering, cook the ramen noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a separate pan, cook the sliced pork or chicken until it is fully cooked and slightly browned.
  4. To assemble the ramen, divide the cooked noodles into bowls. Pour the hot broth over the noodles, making sure to include some of the garlic and ginger for extra flavor.
  5. Top the ramen with the cooked pork or chicken, halved boiled egg, nori strips, menma, and chopped scallions.
  6. Serve immediately and enjoy your delicious bowl of ramen!
