Tteok-bokki

Tteok-bokki is a popular Korean street food made from soft rice cakes, fish cakes, and a sweet and spicy sauce. The dish is typically cooked in a gochujang-based sauce, which gives it its signature red color and bold flavor. Tteok-bokki is often enjoyed as a snack or light meal and is commonly found at street food stalls throughout South Korea. It can be customized with various ingredients such as boiled eggs, vegetables, and cheese to suit different tastes.

  1. 200g tteok (Korean rice cakes)
  2. 100g fish cakes, sliced
  3. 2 cups water
  4. 2 tablespoons gochujang (Korean red chili paste)
  5. 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  6. 1 tablespoon sugar
  7. 1 clove garlic, minced

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, combine the water, gochujang, soy sauce, sugar, and minced garlic. Bring the mixture to a boil, stirring to dissolve the gochujang and sugar.
  2. Add the tteok and fish cakes to the pot. Reduce the heat to medium and simmer for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the tteok becomes soft and the sauce thickens.
  3. Once the tteok is tender and the sauce has reached your desired consistency, remove the pot from the heat. Serve the tteok-bokki hot, garnished with chopped green onions or sesame seeds if desired.
